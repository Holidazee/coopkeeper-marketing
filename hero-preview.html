the honest bit

Spreadsheets don't last. Notebooks get rained on.

Google Sheets get opened twice then forgotten. When I got my first six hens in spring '24, I tried all three. By October I couldn't tell you which hen was molting, when I last bought feed, or whether I was losing money on eggs.

So I built what I actually needed. Open it, tap in today's eggs, close it. It remembers your coop, keeps your feed and cleaning in one place, and over time shows you what your flock is really producing.

— Tay, who is still bad at remembering cleaning days

before you ask

Questions real people have asked me.

Is it actually free? +

Yes. Right now the whole thing is free — I'm one person paying about $7/month to host it. Somewhere down the road there may be paid features for folks who want extras, but the basics — logging eggs, tracking feed and costs, cleaning reminders — will stay free forever.

Is there an app? +

Not yet. It's a website — open it on any phone, tablet, or laptop. Add it to your home screen and it acts like an app. Someday there may be a proper native app, but for now this works just fine.

Will you sell my data? +

No. No third-party trackers, no ad pixels, no email list sold to Tractor Supply.

Do you take feature requests? +

Yes, enthusiastically. Email me at hello@coopkeeper.net — I read every one. A lot of what ends up getting built (including the "maybe someday" stuff up there — a native app, paid extras, whatever) will get built because enough people wrote in and asked for it. So if there's something you want, tell me. I won't build everything, but the ones I hear three times tend to happen.
